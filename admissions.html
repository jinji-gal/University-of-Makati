Qualifications

Senior High School

QUALIFICATIONS

  • New Incoming Grade 11
  • Current Grade 10 students or Junior High School graduates may apply
  • ALTERNATIVE LEARNING SYSTEM PASSERS
    Students who passed the ALS Accreditation and Equivalency (A&E) Test and are eligible to transfer to Senior High School as indicated in the ALS Certificate of Rating (COR)
  • PERSONS WITH DISABILITY (PWD)
    Persons with Disability (PWD) registered under the City of Makati may apply but are subject to screening by the UMak Center for Inclusive Education, Gender, and Development (CIEGAD)
  • Note: The University prioritizes Makati voters or those graduates of any Makati Junior High School whose Grade 9 GWA is 88 or better. Those whose Grade 9 GWA is 85 to 87.99 will be put on the waiting list. Only the top 1500 applicants will be considered.

College

QUALIFICATIONS

  • NEW FRESHMEN
    Student-Applicants who are in Grade 12 this academic year.
  • ALTERNATIVE LEARNING SYSTEM PASSERS
    Student-Applicants who passed the ALS Accreditation and Equivalency (A&E) Test and are eligible for college admission as indicated in the ALS Certificate of Rating
  • TRANSFEREES
    Student-Applicants who have enrolled or taken college units from a different college or university.
    - New-Curriculum students with a Senior High School diploma.
    - Old-Curriculum students must have completed at least two (2) years or four (4) semesters in college with:
     - Met the Graded Weight Average (GWA) and other requirements of the College
     - No accumulated failing grades, Unofficially Dropped (UD), or Incomplete (Inc.) exceeding nine (9) units in one semester or fifteen (15) units total.
     - Be an incoming third-year student.
  • SECOND COURSERS
    Students who have completed a degree/program and wish to pursue another degree in the University and have met the grade requirements of the college.
  • PERSONS WITH DISABILITY (PWD)
    Persons with Disability (PWD) registered under the City of Makati may apply but are subject to screening by the UMak Center for Inclusive Education, Gender, and Development (CIEGAD)
